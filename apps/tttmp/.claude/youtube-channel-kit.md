### Video 1: IRS Tax Detective

**Title:** IRS Tax Detective — Can You Decode These Transaction Codes? | Tax Tools Arcade
**Duration:** ~2 minutes
**Look:** 4

**Script:**

Have you ever looked at an IRS transcript and wondered what all those three-digit codes mean? Code eight-four-six, code nine-seven-one, code five-seven-zero — they look like random numbers, but each one tells a specific story about what's happening with a tax account.

IRS Tax Detective is a matching game that trains you to decode these codes on sight. You'll see a transaction code and need to match it to the right description. Is eight-four-six a refund issued or a payment received? Is five-seven-zero a hold on the account or an audit flag?

The game starts easy — common codes you'll see on almost every transcript. Then it gets harder, mixing in less common codes and adding time pressure. By the time you finish, you'll read transaction codes the way you read plain English.

This is a two-token starter game — great for getting your feet wet. Tax professionals use it to train their team. Taxpayers use it to understand what they're looking at when they pull their own transcript.

Try it yourself — the link is in the description.

### Video 2: Tax Mythbusters Quiz

**Title:** Tax Mythbusters — How Many of These Tax Myths Do You Believe? | Tax Tools Arcade
**Duration:** ~2 minutes
**Look:** 4

**Script:**

Getting a big refund means you did your taxes right. If you file an extension, you're more likely to get audited. Tip income under a certain amount doesn't need to be reported. True or false?

If you hesitated on any of those, you need to play Tax Mythbusters. It's a true-or-false quiz that takes the most common tax misconceptions and puts them to the test. Each answer comes with a clear explanation — not just right or wrong, but why.

This is one of our two-token starter games, and it's the one we recommend everyone play first. Tax professionals — send this to your clients before their next appointment. It clears up the misconceptions they always walk in with. Taxpayers — take it yourself. You might be surprised how many myths you've been treating as facts.

Twenty questions. No jargon. Just clarity. Link in the description.

### Video 3: Tax Scavenger Hunt

**Title:** Tax Scavenger Hunt — Can You Find All the Hidden Documents? | Tax Tools Arcade
**Duration:** ~2 minutes
**Look:** 4

**Script:**

Imagine you're sitting at a desk and somewhere in this office are all the tax documents you need to file a return. W-2s, ten-ninety-nines, mortgage interest statements, charitable donation receipts — they're all here, but you have to find them.

Tax Scavenger Hunt turns document identification into a game. You click through an office scene, find hidden documents, and learn what each one is and where it belongs in the filing process. It's hands-on learning — you're not reading about documents, you're hunting for them.

This is a two-token starter game. It's especially useful if you're new to taxes or if you're a tax professional onboarding a new team member who needs to learn the document landscape quickly.

The full office is waiting. Link in the description.

### Video 4: Tax Time Machine

**Title:** Tax Time Machine — How U.S. Taxes Went from Zero to... This | Tax Tools Arcade
**Duration:** ~2 minutes
**Look:** 4

**Script:**

Did you know the United States had no income tax for most of its history? The first federal income tax was a temporary war measure during the Civil War. It took a constitutional amendment — the Sixteenth — to make it permanent. And the tax code has been growing ever since.

Tax Time Machine is an interactive timeline that walks you through over a century of U.S. tax law. You'll see how rates changed, when major deductions were introduced, how wars and recessions shaped the code, and why the system looks the way it does today.

Understanding where the tax system came from helps you understand where it's going. This is a two-token starter game — and it's the one that always surprises people. History has a way of putting today's tax debates in perspective.

Step into the time machine. Link in the description.
